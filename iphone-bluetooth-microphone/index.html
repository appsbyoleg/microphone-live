How to use iPhone as a Bluetooth microphone

If you want the simplest setup, follow these steps.

  1. Pair your Bluetooth device
    Connect your Bluetooth speaker, headphones, or compatible audio device to your iPhone in system settings.
  2. Open Microphone Live + Rec
    Launch the app on your iPhone or iPad.
  3. Select the Bluetooth output
    Choose the connected audio device as your output target.
  4. Enable live microphone mode
    Start sending your voice from the iPhone microphone to the connected Bluetooth device.
  5. Adjust boost or presets
    Shape the sound for karaoke, speech, or a louder voice presence.
  6. Record if needed
    Save your live session while using Bluetooth output for practice or content creation.

Note: Bluetooth latency can vary. Some speakers feel more responsive than others, and wired output is usually better when you need the lowest delay.

Bluetooth latency: the main limitation

The most common issue in a wireless mic setup is delay. That does not mean the setup is bad, but you need the right expectations.

  • Bluetooth can introduce noticeable latency depending on the device.
  • For casual voice use, karaoke, and practice, many users still find it useful.
  • Speaker quality and Bluetooth implementation matter a lot.
  • Wired output is usually the better choice for the lowest possible latency.

FAQ

Can I use my iPhone as a Bluetooth microphone?

Yes. A live microphone app can turn your iPhone into a Bluetooth microphone for speakers or headphones.

How do I connect my iPhone microphone to a Bluetooth speaker?

Pair the Bluetooth speaker, choose it as output in the app, and enable live microphone mode.

Is there latency when using iPhone as a Bluetooth microphone?

Yes. Some Bluetooth devices add delay, and the exact amount depends on the speaker and connection quality.

Can I record while using iPhone as a Bluetooth mic?

Yes. Some apps support live Bluetooth output and recording together.

Does it work for karaoke?

Yes. Karaoke is one of the most common uses for an iPhone Bluetooth microphone setup.

Does it work with JBL, Bose, AirPods, and other Bluetooth devices?

Yes. Many common Bluetooth speakers and audio devices can work with this kind of setup.
